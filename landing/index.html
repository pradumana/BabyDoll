Questions Worth Asking

Is this a rugpull?

The contract has zero hidden functions. No mint-to-anything, no pause, no blacklist. The source code is public and verifiable on Etherscan. Ownership can be renounced after launch. We encourage you to read every line before sending a single dollar.

Why is burning a good thing for me?

When total supply shrinks and demand stays the same — or grows — the price per token goes up. When you hold $BDOLL and the community burns tokens, your bag represents a bigger % of total supply without you spending anything extra.

What is the $100 actually for?

Deploying a smart contract to Ethereum mainnet requires paying gas fees to the network. The current estimate is ~$100. Every cent goes to the Ethereum network — not to us. We keep nothing from the deployment fund.

How much bonus BDOLL do supporters get?

Bonus allocations are announced before deployment. The earlier you're in, the higher your allocation tier. Contact us directly to lock in your spot and get the exact figures.

Can the team print unlimited tokens?

No. The 10,000,000,000 cap is enforced by the contract. The mint function explicitly checks totalSupply() + amount <= MAX_SUPPLY and reverts if you try to exceed it. There is no override.

When does it go live?

As soon as the deployment fund is reached. Founding supporters get the contract address and Uniswap link before anyone else. The countdown is live — watch it.

What wallet do I need?

Any standard Ethereum wallet — MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, Ledger, Rabby. Once deployed, import $BDOLL using the contract address. Symbol and decimals fill in automatically.

What if the token doesn't take off?

Honest answer: meme coins are high risk. There are no guarantees. Only invest what you can afford to lose entirely. The mechanics are solid — the rest depends on community energy and timing.
